Help / FAQ

What is SwissBudget?

SwissBudget is a "money manager". You can use it to gain a better overview of your money. It helps you with saving money for specific or unspecified goals. It also provides useful analysis over your transactions. You can organize your money using tags. Additionally you can create a budget and SwissBudget will help you stay in those guidelines.
The best thing however, is that your transactions are automatically imported by SwissBudget. You will never have to enter any new transaction or so.

How does SwissBudget work?

Transactions

A transaction is the thing that happened on your Bank Account. Either you received money or the transaction was an expense.
Transactions are imported from Mails that your e-banking service sends you. So, as you surely have already heard, you will have to grant this app access to your e-mail account and you also have to enable detailed e-mail notifications for all transactions in your bank account. This app will be reading these notification mails and extracting the transactions from them.
For performance gains, I highly recommend you to use a custom mail folder for the e-banking notifications (create a rule that automatically moves the notifications to the folder).
You can also tell SwissBudget to automatically delete the transaction notification mails from your mail account. This keeps your transactions a bit more safe.
You can alias a transaction or add a note. These values are just for you and your overview of your transactions. They are not included in any calculations.

Tags

You can assign a tag to a transaction. A tag is used to group transactions. Tags are also positions in your budget or savings.
You can assign tags by hand or use keywords to tell SwissBudget which transaction should be mapped to what tag.
A transaction can only be assigned to one tag. But a tag can obviously have multiple transactions.
There are some default tags, but you can customize them entirely.

Keywords

Here it starts to get complicated. Keywords are used to identify your transactions. (the receiver part is analyzed)
So for every imported transaction SwissBudget tries to automatically find a matching tag. To do this, it needs to know when a tag is matching a transaction. This can be done with keywords. If a keyword was found in the receiver of the transaction then the tag is assigned.
Like tags, you can customize keywords.
Since you can customize them, there is the case, that a transaction matches with multiple tags because multiple keywords matched. These cases will be displayed in the transaction page. I recommend you to regularly check these and assign them to the tag that you intended, otherwise you might have inconsistencies in your budget or savings.
When creating keywords, you need to be careful with how you select them. For minimal errors, use a large part of the transaction receiver. For example "Coop Olten Street name". For large a selection of transactions but possibly more errors, use a small part of the transaction receiver, like "zahn".
Don't worry, the UI will guide you through the process and help you to minimanlize issues.
